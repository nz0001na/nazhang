Research Projects

Facial Micro-Expression Analysis in Interview Videos for Autism DiagnosisOct 2022 - May 2023

West Virginia University [Project URL]
Focused on analyzing face micro-expressions of the participants using contrastive learning method in Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule (ADOS) hour-long videos.
Designed a system to classify autism/control groups by extracting micro-expression subtle movements feature via optical flow/attention mechanism/local patch of interest in unsupervised way.
Obtained 97.32% accuracy showing the efficiency of capturing subtle facial muscle changes for autism diagnosis.

Face Dynamics Analysis for Autism Diagnosis on Interview VideosJul 2019 - Aug 2020

West Virginia University [Project URL] [PDF]
Constructed a 3-category system to classify participants in hour-long videos into Autism, Autism Spectrum, and Non-Spectrum by leveraging well-established computer vision tools from spatio-temporal feature (LPQ-TOP) extraction and marginal fisher analysis (MFA) to few-shot learning (FSL) and scene-level fusion of facial dynamics.
Demonstrated the potential of the proposed automatic ASD trait classification system (achieving 91.72 accuracy on the Caltech ADOS video dataset) and the benefits of few-shot learning and scene-level fusion strategy by extensive ablation studies.

Transformer-based Face Morphing and De-MorphingDec 2021 - Sep 2022

West Virginia University [Project URL] [PDF]
Developed a transformer-based semantic image manipulation GAN scheme to do face morphing.
Designed special loss functions (Wing loss, biometric loss, perceptual loss, and pixel-wise mean square error) to support the optimization in latent code learning of an given face image.
Extended the study to face de-morphing task based on a reference image.
Demonstrated its superiority to StyleGAN-based morphing methods.

Face Recognition (FR) and Face Quality AnalysisAug 2016 - Jun 2019

West Virginia University [Project URL] [PDF]
Investigated the relationship between face image quality and performance of deep learning based cross-quality face recognition quantitatively.
Showed that deep models can allow quality changes to some degrees, but not too large, for the test face images.
Proposed one promising direction for deep learning is to further improve its capability in building the relations between face images with large quality gaps.
Gathered a dataset with 356.4K face images of Chinese celebrities crawled online after cleaning.
Presented an overview of existing deep learning based face recognition algorithms and databases. [Project URL] [PDF]
